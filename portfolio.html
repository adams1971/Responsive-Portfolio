Cascade Dry Cured Meats

Cascade dry cured meats are my personal meat curing project. Through the years, I have developed a hankering to create America cured meats, also known in French as Charcuterie or in Italy, Salumi. These techniques are very similar to each other with various differences based on traditions and demographics. The name Cascade Dry Cured Meats refers to several aspects of my process. I only source pigs raised in the PNW or "Cascadia" region. I have forged relationships with local farmers who are committed to the sustainable growth and care of the animals and the land they live on.  Here in the PNW, our western region is surrounded by the Cascade Mountains to the east and Olympic Mountains to the west, cradling the Puget Sound. It is my belief the weather conditions of this region provide a unique quality that can support and enhance the production of cured meats.A few of my favorites and focuses are Culatello, Cascade Ham and Fennel Pollen DC Sausage- Culatello being the king of Italian Salumi is made from the hunch of a pig's hind leg, this specific muscle is covered with a snow white cap of fat on one side covering deep rouge sweet meat. Each piece weighs about 3 pounds when fully cured and dried.


Cedarbrook Lodge Apiary

Honey Bees- the importance of honey bees in our environment is so strong. Bees are responsible for pollination- the process that helps plants grow thus helping to give us life. For this simple reason, I have chosen to help protect and contribute to the honey bee population. Honey bees are being threatened and so it's important for us to help them survive. I manage two separate hives containing over one-hundred thousand bees each. On a warm summer day- the ski is filled with the hard-working troopers searching for the sweet nectar to bring back the hive to store as food and make into honey. The process is a miracle and the bees never stop working and reproducing.


The Gleason Ranch, Brady Washington

Working closely with local ranches- here I am visiting with Rancher Tracey Baker. For over 180 years, her family has managed this land of over 900 acres called The Gleason Ranch. Today, Tracey and her daughter along with one ranch hand manage over 200 head of cattle at a time. The Gleason Ranch is among few 100% Grass Fed Grass Finished ranches in the state of Washington. She is the only rancher that I know of that raised her animals from breeding to harvest all on the same land- if traceability is important to you- then this is a product to consider. But do not let that be the only deciding factor, the quality of her product surpasses any beef that I have worked in over 25 years. Flavor is the key judgement- The Gleason Ranch continues to surpass all cattle raising standards.
